Pork Riblets

What are pork riblets?

Pork riblets are simply pork ribs that are cut very short and cooked for a long time at a low temperature until they are soft and tender. In this recipe we will using flavors inspired by the Hunan Province of China!

Ingredients

    1.5lbs pork riblets (cut into 2" pieces)
    3 tbsp oil
    2 tbsp Shaoxing wine
    2-3 dried chilis
    2 fresh red chilis
    1.5 tbsp light soy sauce
    1/2 tbsp dark soy sauce
    1 tbsp Chinkiang vineagar
    1 tsp sugar
    3-4 cloves of garlic
    1 2" pice of ginger
    1 scallion
    water as needed (around 1.5 cups)
    1 start anise

Steps

  1. Sear the ribs
    • Heat 2-3 tbsp oil in wok over medium-high heat.
    • Add the ribs and sear until golden brown on most sides. (about 5-7 minutes)
    • Push them aside or remove them from the wok temporarily.
  2. Aromatics + deglaze
    • in the same pan add ginger, garlic, dried chili and fresh chili. Stir fry for 30 seconds.
    • Add Shaoxing wine to deglaze, scraping up the brown bits on the bottom of the pan. (the brown bits will add flaor to the sauce)
  3. Braise
    • Return ribs to the pan. (if removed)
    • Add light and dark soy sauce, sugar and vineagar. Stir to coat>
    • Pour in enough water to mostly cover the ribs.
    • Add scallion and star anise.
    • Bring to a boil, then lower to a simmer. Cover and cook for 30-45 minutes, stirring ocasionally.
  4. Reduce the sauce
    • Once the ribs are tender uncover and raise the heat to medium-high.
    • Reduce the liquid until it is a thick and sticky glaze
  5. Taste and adjust
    • Add more vineagar for tand or chili for heat if needed.
    • Serve hot with rice and maybe pickled or stir fried greens.

Enjoy!
